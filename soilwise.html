SOIL TYPE

Crop selection based on soil type is crucial for maximizing agricultural productivity and ensuring sustainable farming practices. In India, the predominant soil types are alluvial, black, red, lateritic, mountain, and desert soils.

Farmers often conduct soil testing to determine the specific characteristics of their soil, including nutrient content and pH levels. This information helps tailor crop choices and appropriate fertilization strategies for optimal yields and sustainability. Additionally, crop rotation and appropriate agricultural practices are essential to maintain soil health and fertility over time.

Here are crops suitable for each major soil type:

*Black Soil (Regur Soil):*
  • Cotton
  • Soybean
  • Sugarcane
  • Castor
  • Millets
  • Pulses (e.g., pigeon pea)
  • Groundnut
*Red Soil:*
  • Wheat
  • Rice
  • Sugarcane
  • Potato
  • Millets
  • Pulses (e.g., chickpea)
  • Groundnut
*Alluvial Soil:*
  • Rice
  • Wheat
  • Sugarcane
  • Cotton
  • Maize
  • Fruits (e.g., citrus fruits)
*Mountain Soil:*
  • Apples
  • Pears
  • Plums
  • Apricots
  • Peaches
  • Potatoes
  • Barley
*Desert Soil:*
  • Pearl millet (Bajra)
  • Guar
  • Mustard
  • Barley
  • Moong Dal (green gram)
  • Fruits (e.g., dates)
